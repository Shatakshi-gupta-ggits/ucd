Comprehensive Guide to UX Research Techniques & Methodologies

What is UX Research?

UX Research is the systematic study of target users and their requirements to add realistic contexts and insights to design processes.

Context of Use

Context of use describes the circumstances under which users interact with a product.

Four Dimensions of Context:

Dimension Description Example
Where Physical location Home, office, commute, outdoor
When Time and frequency Morning routine, during work, emergency
How Devices and methods Mobile, desktop, tablet, voice
Why Motivation and triggers Need-based, habit-based, goal-driven
üìù Exam Question: Explain the four dimensions of context of use. How does context influence UI design for a food delivery app?

Environment of Use

Physical Environment:

  • Lighting conditions (bright sunlight, dim lighting)
  • Noise levels (quiet office, noisy street)
  • Space constraints (cramped subway, spacious desk)
  • Mobility (stationary, walking, moving vehicle)

Social Environment:

  • Alone vs. with others
  • Privacy concerns
  • Social norms and expectations

Technical Environment:

  • Internet connectivity (WiFi, 4G/5G, offline)
  • Device capabilities (screen size, processing power)
  • Platform (iOS, Android, Web)

Contextual Enquiry

Contextual enquiry is a semi-structured interview method where researchers observe and interview users in their natural environment.

Four Key Principles:

1. Context

Go to the user's workplace and observe them in action

2. Partnership

Collaborate with users to understand their work

3. Interpretation

Develop shared understanding with the user

4. Focus

Direct attention to topics relevant to your design goals

User Interviews

User interviews are one-on-one conversations with users to understand their experiences, needs, and motivations.

Types of User Interviews:

üìã Structured Interviews

Fixed set of predetermined questions asked in the same order

Best for: Quantitative data, large samples, consistency

üí¨ Semi-Structured Interviews

Mix of prepared questions with flexibility to explore topics

Best for: Balance of consistency and depth

üó£Ô∏è Unstructured Interviews

Open-ended conversation guided by general topics

Best for: Exploratory research, discovering unknowns

User Interview Best Practices

‚úÖ Do's:

  • Ask open-ended questions (How, What, Why)
  • Listen actively without interrupting
  • Ask follow-up questions for clarification
  • Stay neutral and avoid leading questions
  • Record sessions (with permission)
  • Take detailed notes

‚ùå Don'ts:

  • Don't ask leading questions ("Don't you think this is good?")
  • Don't ask yes/no questions
  • Don't defend your design
  • Don't ask hypothetical questions
  • Don't interview friends or family
üìù Exam Question: Compare structured, semi-structured, and unstructured interviews. When would you use each type? Provide example questions for each.

Scenarios

Scenarios are narrative descriptions of how users interact with a product to accomplish specific goals.

Components of a Good Scenario:

1. Setting: Where and when the scenario takes place
2. Actors: Who is involved (users, other people)
3. Goals: What the user wants to accomplish
4. Actions: Steps the user takes
5. Events: What happens during the interaction

Example Scenario:

Setting: Monday morning, Sarah is commuting to work on the subway

Goal: Order breakfast to be ready when she arrives at the office

Actions: Opens food delivery app ‚Üí Searches "coffee and bagel" ‚Üí Filters by location near office ‚Üí Selects items ‚Üí Schedules delivery for 9:00 AM ‚Üí Completes payment

Outcome: Order confirmed, estimated arrival 9:05 AM

Creating Effective Personas

Step-by-Step Process:

Step 1: Conduct Research

Gather data through interviews, surveys, analytics, and observations

Step 2: Identify Patterns

Look for common behaviors, goals, and pain points across users

Step 3: Group Similar Users

Cluster users with similar characteristics into segments

Step 4: Create Persona Profiles

Develop 3-5 detailed personas representing each segment

Step 5: Validate

Review personas with stakeholders and real users

Step 6: Share & Use

Distribute personas to team and reference in design decisions

Research Ethics & Best Practices

Ethical Considerations:

‚úÖ Informed Consent

Participants must understand what they're agreeing to and can withdraw at any time

üîí Privacy & Confidentiality

Protect participant data and anonymize research findings

‚öñÔ∏è Fair Compensation

Provide appropriate compensation for participants' time

üéØ No Harm

Ensure research doesn't cause physical, psychological, or social harm

Best Practices Checklist:

  • ‚úì Recruit diverse participants representing your user base
  • ‚úì Prepare research materials in advance (scripts, consent forms)
  • ‚úì Test your research protocol with a pilot study
  • ‚úì Record sessions (with permission) for accurate analysis
  • ‚úì Triangulate findings using multiple research methods
  • ‚úì Share findings with stakeholders promptly
  • ‚úì Archive research data securely for future reference

Common UX Research Mistakes

‚ùå Mistake #1: Confirmation Bias

Problem: Only looking for data that confirms existing beliefs

Solution: Stay neutral, ask open-ended questions, consider contradictory data

‚ùå Mistake #2: Small Sample Size

Problem: Drawing conclusions from too few participants

Solution: Follow the rule: 5+ users for usability, 10-15 for interviews, 20+ for surveys

‚ùå Mistake #3: Leading Questions

Problem: Questions that suggest a desired answer

Solution: Use neutral language, ask "How" and "What" questions

‚ùå Mistake #4: Ignoring Context

Problem: Not considering where and how users interact with product

Solution: Conduct contextual research in users' environment

‚ùå Mistake #5: Not Acting on Findings

Problem: Research sits in reports without implementation

Solution: Create actionable recommendations and track implementation
