üîÑ What is Design Lifecycle?

The Design Lifecycle is a structured, iterative process that guides teams through redesigning or creating products from initial research to final testing and launch.

üéØ Why Use Design Lifecycle?

‚úÖ Benefits

  • Structured Approach: Clear phases and deliverables
  • User-Centered: Keeps focus on user needs
  • Risk Reduction: Catch problems early
  • Better Outcomes: Data-driven decisions
  • Team Alignment: Everyone knows the process
  • Measurable: Clear success criteria

üìä When to Use

  • Redesigning: Existing product/feature
  • New Product: Building from scratch
  • Major Updates: Significant changes
  • Problem Solving: Addressing usability issues
  • Innovation: Exploring new ideas

üîç Discovery: Research Methods

1. üë• User Interviews

What: One-on-one conversations with users

Purpose: Understand behaviors, motivations, pain points

Output: Interview transcripts, key insights

2. üìä Surveys & Questionnaires

What: Collect quantitative data from many users

Purpose: Validate assumptions at scale

Output: Statistical data, trends, patterns

3. üî¨ User Observation (Contextual Inquiry)

What: Watch users in their natural environment

Purpose: See actual behavior vs. reported behavior

Output: Behavioral insights, workflow documentation

4. üìà Analytics Review

What: Analyze existing usage data

Purpose: Identify where users struggle

Output: Drop-off points, popular features, user flows

5. üè¢ Stakeholder Interviews

What: Talk to business owners, managers

Purpose: Understand business goals and constraints

Output: Business requirements, success metrics

üîç Discovery: More Methods

6. üîÑ Competitive Analysis

What: Study competitor products

7. üß™ Heuristic Evaluation

8. üìù Content Audit

9. üë§ User Personas Development
